# Password Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this Password Policy is to establish minimum security requirements for creating, managing, and protecting passwords used to access corporate information systems. Passwords are the first line of defense against unauthorized access, data breaches, and identity compromise. This policy aims to reduce cybersecurity risks by enforcing strong authentication practices across all business applications, cloud services, network infrastructure, and endpoint devices.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, interns, temporary staff, third-party vendors, and any individual who has been granted access to company-owned systems or information assets. It covers passwords used for workstation logins, email accounts, databases, VPN connections, cloud platforms, development environments, administrative accounts, and business applications.

Service accounts, privileged administrator accounts, and application credentials are also subject to this policy unless governed by stricter security controls.

## Policy

All user accounts must be protected by strong passwords that contain at least twelve characters. Passwords should include a combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. Users are encouraged to create passphrases consisting of unrelated words to improve memorability while maintaining security.

Passwords must be unique for every corporate system. Reusing passwords across multiple company applications or using the same password for personal accounts is strictly prohibited. A compromise of one system should not expose access to other organizational resources.

Default passwords supplied by hardware vendors, software providers, or cloud services must be changed immediately before systems are placed into production. Temporary passwords issued by the IT department must also be replaced during the first successful login.

User passwords must be changed every ninety days unless stronger authentication mechanisms or approved passwordless technologies have been implemented. Password history controls shall prevent users from reusing recently used passwords.

Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for remote access, privileged accounts, cloud services, financial systems, and applications containing confidential or regulated information. Passwords alone are not considered sufficient protection for sensitive systems.

Employees must never share passwords with colleagues, managers, contractors, or external parties. IT personnel will never request passwords through email, phone calls, chat applications, or support tickets. If such requests are received, employees must report them immediately as potential phishing attempts.

Passwords must not be written on paper, stored in unsecured documents, saved in web browsers on shared devices, or transmitted through unencrypted communication channels. Approved enterprise password managers should be used to securely generate and store complex passwords.

Accounts will automatically lock after five consecutive failed authentication attempts. Locked accounts may only be restored using approved identity verification procedures administered by the Information Technology Service Desk.

Compromised passwords must be changed immediately upon suspicion of unauthorized disclosure. Employees who receive phishing emails, accidentally reveal credentials, or suspect account misuse must notify the Information Security team without delay.

## Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for creating strong passwords, protecting authentication credentials, reporting suspected compromise, and complying with all password management requirements.

Managers are responsible for ensuring team members complete required security awareness training and comply with authentication standards for systems under their responsibility.

The Information Technology department is responsible for implementing technical password controls, enforcing password complexity requirements, managing identity systems, monitoring authentication logs, and responding to password-related security incidents.

Information Security personnel are responsible for conducting periodic audits, reviewing authentication risks, recommending improvements, and investigating suspected credential misuse.

## Exceptions

Service accounts, automated application accounts, and legacy systems that cannot fully comply with password complexity requirements may receive documented exceptions following a formal risk assessment. Approved compensating controls, such as network isolation, privileged access monitoring, credential vaulting, or enhanced logging, must be implemented before any exception is granted.

Emergency administrative access procedures may temporarily bypass standard password rotation requirements but must be documented, approved, monitored, and reviewed after the emergency has concluded.

## Compliance

Compliance with this policy is mandatory for all authorized users. Automated security tools may continuously monitor password strength, authentication failures, privileged account activity, and policy adherence.

Failure to comply with this policy may result in password resets, temporary suspension of user accounts, revocation of system access, disciplinary action, or legal consequences where applicable. The Information Security team will periodically review this policy to ensure alignment with evolving cybersecurity threats, regulatory obligations, and industry best practices.
