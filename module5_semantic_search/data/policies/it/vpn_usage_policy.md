# VPN Usage Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this VPN Usage Policy is to establish secure standards for accessing corporate systems from remote locations. A Virtual Private Network (VPN) encrypts network traffic between user devices and company infrastructure, reducing the risk of unauthorized interception, credential theft, and data exposure. This policy defines acceptable use, security requirements, and user responsibilities for VPN access.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, third-party vendors, and authorized users who access company resources from outside corporate offices. It covers remote access to internal applications, cloud services, file servers, development environments, databases, email systems, and administrative platforms using company-approved VPN solutions.

Users connecting from home, customer locations, hotels, airports, or other external networks must comply with this policy.

## Policy

Remote access to corporate resources must be performed exclusively through the organization's approved VPN service. Direct access to internal systems over the public internet is prohibited unless specifically approved by the Information Security department.

VPN access is granted only after user identity has been verified and business justification has been approved by the employee's manager and the Information Technology department. Access permissions are assigned according to the principle of least privilege, ensuring users receive only the minimum level of access required to perform their job responsibilities.

Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for every VPN login. Users must authenticate using their corporate credentials and an approved second authentication factor before access is granted.

Only company-managed devices that comply with corporate security standards may connect to the VPN. Devices must have updated operating systems, approved antivirus software, endpoint detection tools, disk encryption, and current security patches. Personally owned devices may only be used if specifically authorized under the organization's Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) policy.

VPN sessions are encrypted using approved cryptographic protocols. Employees must not attempt to disable encryption, modify VPN configurations, bypass security controls, or use unauthorized VPN software for corporate access.

Split tunneling is disabled by default to ensure all business-related network traffic passes through corporate security controls, including firewalls, intrusion detection systems, web filtering, and security monitoring solutions.

Users must disconnect from the VPN when remote access is no longer required. Persistent or unattended VPN connections should be avoided unless specifically required for approved business operations.

Employees must immediately report suspected credential compromise, unauthorized VPN access, unusual login notifications, lost company devices, or any indication that their account may have been misused.

The Information Technology department reserves the right to temporarily suspend VPN access during security investigations, malware outbreaks, system maintenance, or suspected policy violations to protect organizational assets.

## Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for protecting their authentication credentials, using approved devices, maintaining secure home or remote networks where possible, and following all security requirements defined in this policy.

Managers are responsible for reviewing business justification before approving VPN access and periodically validating that remote access remains necessary for each employee.

The Information Technology department is responsible for maintaining VPN infrastructure, managing user accounts, monitoring connection availability, applying security updates, and supporting authorized users experiencing technical issues.

The Information Security team is responsible for monitoring VPN activity, reviewing authentication logs, investigating suspicious connections, identifying unauthorized access attempts, and recommending improvements to remote access security.

## Exceptions

Temporary exceptions may be granted for business-critical situations involving external vendors, emergency response teams, or legacy systems that require specialized connectivity. All exceptions must undergo documented risk assessment and receive approval from Information Security management.

Alternative remote access solutions may only be implemented after formal security review and written authorization. Compensating security controls must provide protection equivalent to or greater than the standard VPN solution.

## Compliance

VPN connection logs, authentication events, device compliance status, and access records may be monitored and audited to ensure adherence to this policy. Users should have no expectation of privacy when accessing corporate resources through company-managed VPN infrastructure.

Failure to comply with this policy may result in immediate suspension of VPN privileges, revocation of remote access permissions, disciplinary action, or termination of employment depending on the severity of the violation. The organization will periodically review this policy to ensure continued compliance with cybersecurity standards, regulatory requirements, and evolving business needs.
