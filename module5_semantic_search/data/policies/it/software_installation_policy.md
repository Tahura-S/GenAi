# Software Installation Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this Software Installation Policy is to ensure that all software installed on company-owned devices is secure, legally licensed, compatible with organizational standards, and aligned with business requirements. Unauthorized or unverified software can introduce malware, security vulnerabilities, licensing violations, and operational instability. This policy establishes standardized procedures for requesting, approving, installing, updating, and removing software across the organization.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, interns, temporary staff, and third-party users who utilize company-owned desktops, laptops, virtual machines, servers, mobile devices, or cloud-based computing environments.

The policy covers commercial software, open-source applications, internally developed tools, browser extensions, mobile applications, utilities, development frameworks, command-line tools, and software obtained through cloud-based software-as-a-service (SaaS) platforms.

## Policy

Only software approved by the Information Technology department may be installed on company-managed devices. Employees must not independently download or install applications from the internet, removable media, or third-party websites without prior authorization.

Requests for new software must be submitted through the organization's IT Service Portal. Each request should include the business justification, intended use, software publisher, licensing requirements, expected users, and any integration dependencies. The IT department will evaluate compatibility, security risks, licensing obligations, and operational impact before granting approval.

All software must originate from trusted and verified sources. Applications downloaded from unofficial repositories, unknown websites, peer-to-peer networks, or unverified distribution channels are strictly prohibited. Software integrity may be validated through digital signatures, vendor verification, or checksum validation before deployment.

Commercial software must be properly licensed in accordance with vendor agreements. Employees must not install pirated software, unauthorized copies, cracked applications, or software that violates intellectual property rights. The organization maintains centralized records of software licenses and asset inventories to support compliance audits.

Open-source software may be used only after technical and security review by the Information Technology and Information Security teams. The review should consider software maintenance activity, dependency risks, known vulnerabilities, community support, license compatibility, and long-term sustainability.

Administrative privileges required for software installation are restricted to authorized IT personnel. Employees must not attempt to bypass operating system protections, disable endpoint security tools, or use privilege escalation methods to install unauthorized software.

The Information Technology department is responsible for deploying software updates, security patches, and version upgrades according to established maintenance schedules. Critical security updates should be prioritized to reduce exposure to known vulnerabilities.

Software that is no longer required, unsupported by the vendor, reaches end-of-life status, or presents unacceptable security risks may be removed from company devices without prior notice. Where operationally feasible, users will be informed before removal.

Periodic software inventory scans may be conducted to identify unauthorized applications, outdated versions, unsupported software, duplicate installations, and licensing inconsistencies. Systems found to contain prohibited software may be isolated from the corporate network until remediation is completed.

## Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for requesting software through approved processes, using applications only for legitimate business purposes, reporting software issues, and avoiding unauthorized installations.

Managers are responsible for validating business justification for software requests and ensuring requested applications support departmental objectives while remaining cost-effective.

The Information Technology department is responsible for evaluating software requests, maintaining approved software catalogs, managing software deployment, monitoring license compliance, applying security updates, and maintaining software asset inventories.

The Information Security team is responsible for assessing software-related cybersecurity risks, reviewing vulnerabilities, monitoring emerging threats, and recommending appropriate security controls before software approval.

## Exceptions

Exceptions may be granted for research activities, software development, testing environments, proof-of-concept projects, or specialized business applications that require non-standard software. All exceptions must undergo documented security review, management approval, and periodic reassessment.

Legacy applications required for operational continuity may remain in use where replacement is not immediately feasible. Such systems must be protected using compensating controls such as network segmentation, restricted user access, enhanced monitoring, or virtualization.

## Compliance

Compliance with this policy is mandatory for all users of company technology resources. The organization may conduct periodic audits to verify software licensing, installation records, security compliance, and asset inventory accuracy.

Unauthorized software installations, licensing violations, deliberate circumvention of security controls, or repeated non-compliance may result in removal of software, suspension of administrative privileges, disciplinary action, revocation of system access, or termination of employment depending on the severity of the violation.

This policy will be reviewed periodically by the Information Technology and Information Security departments to ensure continued alignment with business objectives, cybersecurity best practices, regulatory requirements, and evolving technology standards.
