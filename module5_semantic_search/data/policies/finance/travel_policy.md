# Travel Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this Travel Policy is to establish standardized procedures for planning, approving, booking, and reimbursing business travel. The organization recognizes that travel is sometimes necessary to support customer engagement, business development, training, project implementation, audits, and operational activities. This policy promotes responsible spending, employee safety, regulatory compliance, and efficient use of company resources while ensuring business travel is conducted consistently across all departments.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, executives, contractors, and authorized representatives traveling on behalf of the organization. It covers domestic and international travel, including transportation, accommodation, meals, local travel, visa expenses, travel insurance, conference participation, and other approved business-related travel costs.

Personal travel combined with official business travel is permitted only with prior approval and must not increase costs to the organization.

## Policy

All business travel must receive approval from the employee's reporting manager before any reservations or financial commitments are made. International travel or travel exceeding approved budget thresholds may require additional approval from department leadership or Finance.

Employees should use the organization's approved travel management platform or authorized travel agency when booking flights, hotels, rental vehicles, or rail transportation. Direct bookings outside approved channels should only occur when authorized or when operationally necessary.

Travel arrangements should prioritize cost-effectiveness while maintaining reasonable comfort, productivity, and employee safety. Employees are expected to book transportation and accommodation sufficiently in advance to obtain competitive pricing whenever practical.

Economy class is the standard for domestic travel and international flights below six hours. Premium travel classes may be approved for long-haul international flights, medical accommodations, or other exceptional business circumstances with appropriate management authorization.

Hotel accommodations should be selected based on reasonable pricing, proximity to the business location, employee safety, and availability. Luxury hotels should be avoided unless they are the designated venue for a business event or no practical alternatives exist.

Employees may claim reimbursement for transportation expenses including airfare, rail travel, taxis, ride-sharing services, airport transfers, parking fees, toll charges, fuel expenses for approved personal vehicle usage, and public transportation when directly related to business activities.

Meal expenses are reimbursable within established daily allowance limits. Employees should exercise reasonable judgment and avoid excessive or extravagant spending. Alcoholic beverages are generally not reimbursable unless specifically approved for authorized client entertainment or official corporate events.

International travelers must obtain required passports, visas, work permits, vaccinations, and travel documentation before departure. The organization may reimburse approved visa processing fees, mandatory travel insurance, and required government charges.

Employees are responsible for protecting company equipment, confidential information, and business records while traveling. Public Wi-Fi networks should only be used in accordance with the organization's cybersecurity policies, and remote access to corporate systems must be performed through approved VPN services.

Travel disruptions, emergencies, accidents, lost equipment, or security concerns should be reported immediately to the employee's manager and the appropriate organizational support contacts.

## Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for planning travel responsibly, obtaining required approvals, maintaining supporting documentation, complying with applicable travel regulations, protecting company assets, and submitting travel expense reports within prescribed timelines.

Managers are responsible for reviewing travel requests, evaluating business necessity, ensuring budget availability, approving appropriate travel arrangements, and monitoring departmental travel expenditures.

The Finance department is responsible for reviewing travel-related expense claims, processing reimbursements, maintaining financial records, monitoring travel costs, and ensuring compliance with organizational budgeting policies.

The Human Resources department may provide assistance related to employee welfare during business travel, while the Information Technology department supports secure remote access and protection of company devices used during travel.

## Exceptions

Emergency travel resulting from customer incidents, operational disruptions, regulatory requirements, natural disasters, medical emergencies, or other unforeseen business events may require expedited approvals and alternative travel arrangements.

Exceptions to standard travel class, accommodation limits, or booking procedures may be approved when justified by employee health, accessibility requirements, business continuity, or significant operational considerations. All exceptions should be documented and approved by authorized management.

## Compliance

Travel bookings, approvals, expense reports, and supporting documentation are subject to periodic review by the Finance department and Internal Audit. Employees must ensure all submitted information is accurate, complete, and supported by appropriate evidence.

Failure to comply with this policy, submission of fraudulent travel claims, unauthorized travel commitments, misuse of company funds, or repeated violations may result in denial of reimbursement, disciplinary action, recovery of excess payments, suspension of travel privileges, or termination of employment depending on the severity of the violation.

This policy will be reviewed periodically to reflect changes in business operations, travel risks, financial controls, regulatory requirements, and organizational objectives.
