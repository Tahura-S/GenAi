# Procurement Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this Procurement Policy is to establish a structured and transparent process for acquiring goods, services, software, equipment, and professional consulting services required by the organization. The policy promotes responsible financial management, fair vendor selection, regulatory compliance, and effective risk management while ensuring procurement activities support organizational objectives and provide value for money.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, managers, procurement personnel, executives, project teams, and authorized representatives involved in requesting, approving, purchasing, receiving, or managing goods and services on behalf of the organization.

The policy covers purchases of office supplies, information technology equipment, cloud services, software licenses, professional services, facility maintenance, consulting engagements, capital assets, and long-term contractual agreements.

## Policy

All procurement activities shall follow the principles of transparency, fairness, accountability, competition, and cost effectiveness. Purchasing decisions should be based on business requirements, total cost of ownership, supplier reliability, quality standards, delivery capability, information security, and contractual obligations rather than personal preference.

Employees requiring goods or services must submit a formal purchase request through the organization's procurement system before making any financial commitment. Purchase requests should clearly describe the business need, technical specifications, estimated budget, required delivery date, preferred suppliers if applicable, and supporting business justification.

Procurement personnel will review submitted requests to verify budget availability, purchasing requirements, supplier eligibility, and compliance with organizational procurement procedures. Requests may be returned for clarification if documentation is incomplete or inconsistent.

Purchases exceeding established financial thresholds require competitive vendor quotations. Unless an approved exception applies, at least three qualified suppliers should be evaluated. Vendor selection should consider pricing, product quality, warranty terms, delivery schedules, service capability, regulatory compliance, cybersecurity requirements where applicable, and overall business value.

Approved suppliers should be selected from the organization's authorized vendor database whenever practical. The onboarding of new suppliers requires completion of due diligence procedures, including financial assessment, legal verification, tax documentation, sanctions screening, and evaluation of information security practices when suppliers will access company systems or confidential information.

Purchase Orders (POs) must be issued before suppliers begin providing goods or services unless alternative procurement procedures have been formally approved. Employees must not make verbal purchasing commitments, sign supplier agreements, or authorize work beyond their delegated financial authority.

Contracts involving software subscriptions, cloud services, consulting engagements, or recurring business services must undergo review by the Legal, Finance, Information Technology, and Information Security departments where appropriate. Contract approval should address commercial terms, service level agreements, confidentiality obligations, intellectual property ownership, data protection requirements, and termination conditions.

Upon delivery, requesting departments are responsible for verifying that received goods or completed services conform to purchase specifications before authorizing payment. Any shortages, defects, damaged products, or service deficiencies should be reported promptly to Procurement for supplier resolution.

Invoices will only be processed after successful three-way matching between the approved Purchase Order, supplier invoice, and goods receipt or service confirmation. Payment discrepancies must be investigated before financial settlement.

## Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for submitting accurate purchase requests, avoiding unauthorized purchases, providing complete business justification, and confirming satisfactory receipt of goods or services.

Department managers are responsible for reviewing procurement requests, validating operational necessity, confirming budget availability, and ensuring purchases align with departmental objectives.

The Procurement department is responsible for managing supplier relationships, conducting competitive sourcing activities, negotiating commercial terms, maintaining approved vendor records, monitoring supplier performance, and ensuring compliance with procurement regulations.

The Finance department is responsible for verifying budget availability, processing approved payments, maintaining financial records, supporting procurement audits, and monitoring purchasing expenditures.

The Legal and Information Security departments are responsible for reviewing contracts involving legal obligations, confidential information, cybersecurity risks, and regulatory compliance.

## Exceptions

Emergency procurement may be authorized when immediate purchasing is necessary to address operational disruptions, safety incidents, regulatory requirements, cybersecurity emergencies, or business continuity events. Emergency purchases must be documented, justified, and reviewed after the immediate situation has been resolved.

Single-source procurement may be approved where only one qualified supplier exists, compatibility with existing infrastructure is required, intellectual property restrictions apply, or specialized expertise cannot reasonably be obtained through competitive bidding.

## Compliance

Procurement activities are subject to periodic review by Finance, Internal Audit, Compliance, and external auditors where applicable. Procurement records, supplier evaluations, purchase approvals, contracts, invoices, and payment documentation must be retained according to the organization's record retention requirements.

Unauthorized purchasing, invoice splitting to avoid approval thresholds, acceptance of inappropriate gifts from suppliers, conflicts of interest, fraudulent procurement activities, or deliberate circumvention of procurement controls may result in disciplinary action, recovery of financial losses, termination of employment, cancellation of supplier contracts, and legal proceedings where appropriate.

This policy shall be reviewed periodically to ensure continued alignment with organizational governance standards, financial controls, regulatory obligations, and industry best practices.
