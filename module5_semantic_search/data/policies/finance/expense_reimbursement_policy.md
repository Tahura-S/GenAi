# Expense Reimbursement Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this Expense Reimbursement Policy is to establish consistent procedures for reimbursing employees for legitimate business expenses incurred while performing authorized company activities. The policy promotes financial accountability, accurate record keeping, regulatory compliance, and responsible use of organizational resources. It also ensures that reimbursement requests are processed fairly, transparently, and within established financial controls.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, managers, executives, and authorized personnel who incur business-related expenses on behalf of the organization. Contractors and consultants may be reimbursed only when explicitly permitted under their contractual agreements.

The policy covers expenses related to business travel, client meetings, training programs, conferences, office supplies, communication costs, local transportation, accommodation, meals, and other approved business activities.

## Policy

Employees should use company-issued payment methods whenever available. Personal funds should only be used when a corporate payment option is unavailable or impractical. All reimbursable expenses must be reasonable, necessary, directly related to business operations, and supported by appropriate documentation.

Expense claims must be submitted through the organization's expense management system within fifteen calendar days after the expense is incurred. Claims submitted after the prescribed deadline may be delayed, rejected, or require additional management approval unless exceptional circumstances are documented.

Original receipts, invoices, electronic payment confirmations, or equivalent supporting documents are required for every reimbursable expense. Documentation must clearly identify the vendor, purchase date, items or services acquired, applicable taxes, currency, and total amount paid.

Business meals may be reimbursed when conducted for legitimate business purposes such as customer meetings, employee recruitment, project discussions, or approved team activities. Expense reports should identify the business purpose, meeting participants, and associated project or department whenever applicable.

Travel-related expenses including airfare, rail transportation, accommodation, taxi services, rideshare services, parking fees, toll charges, and public transportation are reimbursable only when they comply with the organization's Travel Policy and have received appropriate approval.

Personal expenses, family expenses, entertainment unrelated to business activities, traffic fines, parking violations, luxury purchases, alcoholic beverages unless specifically authorized, and expenses lacking adequate documentation are not eligible for reimbursement.

Currency conversion for international expenses shall be calculated using the official exchange rate recognized by the organization's finance system or the exchange rate charged by the payment provider. Employees should retain documentation supporting foreign currency transactions.

Managers reviewing expense claims should verify that submitted expenses are reasonable, accurately documented, comply with applicable policies, and align with approved departmental budgets. Approval should be based on business necessity rather than employee seniority.

The Finance department reserves the right to request additional supporting documentation, clarification, or business justification before approving reimbursement. Incomplete or inaccurate submissions may be returned for correction before processing.

## Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for exercising sound financial judgment, minimizing unnecessary business expenses, maintaining accurate supporting documentation, and submitting expense reports within established timelines.

Managers are responsible for reviewing expense claims objectively, confirming business relevance, verifying policy compliance, approving legitimate expenses, and rejecting unsupported or excessive claims.

The Finance department is responsible for reviewing approved expense reports, validating supporting documentation, processing reimbursements, maintaining financial records, monitoring budget compliance, and supporting internal and external financial audits.

Internal Audit may periodically review reimbursement transactions to identify policy violations, duplicate claims, fraudulent activity, or opportunities to improve financial controls.

## Exceptions

Exceptional reimbursement requests involving emergency travel, unforeseen operational requirements, natural disasters, medical emergencies, or significant business disruptions may be considered outside normal policy requirements. Such requests require documented justification and approval from both departmental management and the Finance department.

Where receipts are unavailable due to exceptional circumstances, employees may be required to submit a signed declaration together with alternative evidence of payment. Approval of such requests remains at the discretion of Finance.

## Compliance

Expense reimbursement records are subject to financial review, regulatory inspection, tax compliance requirements, and internal audit procedures. Employees must provide truthful, complete, and accurate information when submitting reimbursement claims.

Knowingly submitting false claims, altering receipts, duplicating expenses, inflating reimbursement amounts, or intentionally violating this policy constitutes financial misconduct and may result in reimbursement denial, disciplinary action, termination of employment, recovery of improperly reimbursed funds, and legal action where appropriate.

This policy will be reviewed periodically to ensure continued compliance with accounting standards, tax regulations, organizational financial controls, and evolving business requirements.
