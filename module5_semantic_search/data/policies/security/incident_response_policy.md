# Incident Response Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this Incident Response Policy is to establish a structured process for identifying, reporting, assessing, containing, investigating, recovering from, and documenting information security incidents. Timely and coordinated incident response minimizes operational disruption, protects organizational assets, limits financial and reputational damage, and supports compliance with legal and regulatory obligations.

The organization recognizes that cybersecurity incidents may occur despite preventive security controls. A well-defined response process enables rapid decision-making, effective communication, evidence preservation, and continuous improvement of the organization's security posture.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, third-party service providers, and authorized users who access or manage company information systems.

The policy covers incidents affecting company-owned devices, cloud services, business applications, networks, databases, servers, endpoints, mobile devices, and information assets. It includes suspected or confirmed events such as malware infections, ransomware attacks, phishing campaigns, unauthorized access, credential compromise, insider threats, denial-of-service attacks, data breaches, system misuse, loss of company devices, and accidental disclosure of confidential information.

## Policy

Every employee has a responsibility to report suspected security incidents immediately after discovery. Incidents should be reported to the Information Security team or Service Desk using approved reporting channels. Delayed reporting may increase organizational risk and hinder effective containment.

Upon receiving an incident report, the Information Security team shall perform an initial assessment to determine the nature, scope, severity, and potential business impact of the event. Incidents shall be classified according to established severity levels to support appropriate escalation and resource allocation.

Where necessary, immediate containment measures may include isolating affected devices, disabling compromised user accounts, blocking malicious network traffic, restricting system access, revoking credentials, or temporarily suspending vulnerable services. Containment actions should minimize operational disruption while preventing further compromise.

Evidence associated with security incidents shall be preserved in a manner that supports forensic investigation and potential legal proceedings. System logs, audit records, memory captures, network traffic, emails, and other relevant evidence should be collected using approved procedures while maintaining chain-of-custody documentation.

The Incident Response Team shall coordinate investigation activities to identify the root cause, affected assets, attack methods, exploited vulnerabilities, and potential impact on organizational operations. External cybersecurity specialists or law enforcement agencies may be engaged when required.

Communication during security incidents shall follow established escalation procedures. Employees must not disclose incident details to customers, suppliers, media representatives, or external organizations unless authorized by executive management, Legal, or Corporate Communications.

Following containment and investigation, recovery activities shall restore affected systems using verified backups, validated configurations, approved security patches, and strengthened security controls. Restored systems should undergo testing before returning to normal production operations.

Every significant incident shall conclude with a documented post-incident review. Lessons learned should identify process improvements, security control enhancements, user awareness requirements, technology upgrades, and recommendations to reduce the likelihood of similar incidents occurring in the future.

## Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for recognizing potential security incidents, reporting suspicious activity promptly, protecting evidence where appropriate, and cooperating fully during incident investigations.

Managers are responsible for supporting incident response activities within their departments, ensuring employees follow reporting procedures, assisting with operational decision-making, and facilitating business continuity during significant disruptions.

The Information Security team is responsible for incident assessment, containment, forensic investigation, recovery coordination, documentation, threat analysis, and continuous improvement of incident response capabilities.

The Information Technology department is responsible for restoring affected infrastructure, applying security updates, rebuilding compromised systems, maintaining backup services, and implementing technical remediation measures.

The Legal, Compliance, Human Resources, and Executive Leadership teams provide guidance regarding regulatory reporting, employee investigations, contractual obligations, public communications, and business risk management where applicable.

## Exceptions

Emergency situations involving immediate threats to employee safety, critical infrastructure, or business continuity may require accelerated response procedures. Such actions must be documented and reviewed after the incident has been resolved.

Where contractual agreements or regulatory obligations require external notification within defined timeframes, incident response activities shall prioritize compliance with those requirements while preserving evidence and maintaining operational stability.

## Compliance

Incident reports, investigation records, forensic evidence, recovery documentation, communication logs, and post-incident reviews shall be retained in accordance with the organization's Data Retention Policy and applicable legal requirements.

Failure to report known security incidents, intentional concealment of cybersecurity events, unauthorized disclosure of investigation information, destruction of evidence, or deliberate interference with incident response activities may result in disciplinary action, revocation of system access, termination of employment, legal proceedings, or regulatory penalties where applicable.

This policy shall be reviewed periodically following significant security incidents, technology changes, regulatory updates, or annual security governance reviews to ensure continued effectiveness and alignment with industry best practices.
