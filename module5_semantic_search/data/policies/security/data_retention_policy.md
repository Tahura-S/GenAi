# Data Retention Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this Data Retention Policy is to establish consistent requirements for retaining, archiving, and securely disposing of organizational information throughout its lifecycle. Effective data retention supports legal compliance, operational efficiency, business continuity, regulatory reporting, and information security while reducing risks associated with unnecessary storage of sensitive or outdated information.

This policy ensures that business records remain available for legitimate operational, legal, audit, and regulatory purposes while preventing unauthorized access to information that no longer requires retention.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, third-party service providers, and business units that create, process, store, archive, or dispose of organizational information.

The policy covers both physical and electronic records, including employee records, customer information, financial documents, contracts, emails, invoices, purchase orders, audit reports, security logs, application data, databases, backup media, cloud storage, collaboration platforms, and system-generated records.

All information assets owned, processed, or managed by the organization are subject to this policy regardless of storage location.

## Policy

Information shall be retained only for the period necessary to satisfy business requirements, contractual obligations, legal requirements, regulatory obligations, or operational needs. Once the approved retention period expires, information should be securely archived or permanently disposed of using approved destruction methods.

Business records shall be classified according to the organization's Information Classification Policy. Retention periods may differ depending on the sensitivity, legal significance, and operational importance of the information being maintained.

Financial records, accounting documents, tax filings, invoices, purchase records, payroll documentation, and supporting audit materials shall normally be retained for a minimum of seven years unless local legislation requires a longer retention period.

Employee records, including employment agreements, payroll information, performance evaluations, disciplinary records, training records, and benefits documentation, shall be retained in accordance with applicable employment laws and organizational human resource requirements.

Customer information shall be retained only for legitimate business purposes, contractual obligations, regulatory compliance, customer support, dispute resolution, or legal requirements. Personal information should not be retained indefinitely once business purposes have been fulfilled.

Email communications that constitute official business records should be retained according to their business function rather than their communication format. Routine administrative messages with no continuing business value may be deleted according to approved operational procedures.

System logs, authentication records, security monitoring data, firewall logs, VPN connection records, and cybersecurity event logs shall be retained for periods defined by the Information Security department to support incident investigation, forensic analysis, regulatory compliance, and threat detection.

Backup copies shall be encrypted and stored in secure on-site or off-site facilities that meet organizational security standards. Backup retention schedules should align with disaster recovery objectives and business continuity requirements while avoiding unnecessary duplication of obsolete information.

When information reaches the end of its approved retention period, disposal shall be performed using secure methods appropriate for the storage medium. Electronic data should be securely erased using approved data sanitization techniques, while paper records containing confidential information should be cross-shredded or destroyed through certified document destruction services.

If litigation, regulatory investigation, audit activities, or legal preservation requirements exist, affected information must not be altered, deleted, or destroyed until the legal hold has been formally released.

## Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for creating accurate business records, storing information in approved systems, following applicable retention schedules, and avoiding unnecessary duplication of organizational data.

Department managers are responsible for ensuring business records under their supervision are maintained appropriately and disposed of according to approved retention requirements.

The Information Technology department is responsible for maintaining secure storage infrastructure, backup systems, archival solutions, and technical controls supporting information lifecycle management.

The Information Security department is responsible for defining secure disposal standards, monitoring compliance, protecting archived information, and supporting investigations involving retained records.

The Legal and Compliance departments are responsible for establishing legal retention requirements, issuing legal hold notices, interpreting regulatory obligations, and advising departments regarding record preservation.

## Exceptions

Exceptions to established retention periods may be approved when required by applicable law, contractual obligations, pending litigation, regulatory investigations, customer agreements, or documented business requirements. All exceptions must be formally documented and approved by authorized management.

Legacy systems containing historical information that cannot immediately comply with current retention requirements may remain operational temporarily while migration or remediation plans are implemented.

## Compliance

Compliance with this policy is mandatory for all personnel handling organizational information. Periodic audits may be conducted to verify adherence to retention schedules, secure disposal practices, archival controls, and legal preservation requirements.

Failure to retain required records, unauthorized destruction of information, improper handling of confidential records, or intentional violation of legal hold requirements may result in disciplinary action, regulatory penalties, legal liability, financial losses, or termination of employment depending on the severity of the violation.

This policy will be reviewed periodically to ensure continued alignment with applicable laws, regulatory standards, cybersecurity best practices, and evolving business requirements.
