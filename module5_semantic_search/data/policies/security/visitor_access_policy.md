# Visitor Access Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this Visitor Access Policy is to establish standardized procedures for managing visitors entering company facilities while protecting employees, information assets, physical infrastructure, and business operations. Effective visitor management reduces the risk of unauthorized access, theft, workplace incidents, and accidental exposure of confidential information. This policy ensures that all visitors are identified, authorized, monitored, and escorted appropriately throughout their visit.

## Scope

This policy applies to all visitors entering company-owned or leased facilities, including customers, suppliers, contractors, consultants, auditors, maintenance personnel, delivery personnel, interview candidates, regulatory representatives, and guests attending business meetings or company-sponsored events.

The policy applies to corporate offices, data centers, research facilities, warehouses, manufacturing locations, and any other premises managed by the organization.

## Policy

All visitors must report to the designated reception or security desk immediately upon arrival. Entry through employee-only entrances without prior authorization is strictly prohibited.

Visitors must present a valid government-issued photo identification or other approved identification document before access is granted. Security personnel may verify visitor identity against appointment records, approved visitor lists, or host confirmation before issuing access credentials.

Employees hosting visitors are responsible for registering the visit in advance whenever reasonably possible. Advance registration should include the visitor's name, organization, expected arrival time, business purpose, meeting location, and anticipated departure time.

Each visitor shall receive a temporary visitor badge that clearly identifies them as a non-employee. Visitor badges must remain visible at all times while inside company premises and must be returned to security upon departure.

Visitors may only access locations necessary for their approved business purpose. Entry into restricted areas such as server rooms, network operations centers, executive offices, research laboratories, records storage facilities, financial operations areas, and security control rooms requires additional authorization.

Visitors must remain under the supervision of their designated host while inside controlled areas. Employees are responsible for ensuring that visitors do not enter unauthorized locations or gain access to confidential information beyond what is required for legitimate business activities.

Confidential documents, computer systems, whiteboards, printed reports, prototypes, customer information, and intellectual property should not be left unattended or visible in meeting rooms where visitors are present unless disclosure has been formally authorized.

Photography, video recording, audio recording, or use of drones within company facilities is prohibited unless written approval has been granted by authorized management. Security personnel may inspect visitor equipment when required to protect organizational assets.

Contractors performing maintenance or technical services requiring extended facility access may receive temporary contractor credentials after completing required security verification, safety orientation, and confidentiality documentation.

Delivery personnel should remain within designated delivery zones unless escorted by authorized employees. Packages entering secure facilities may be subject to inspection according to organizational security procedures.

## Responsibilities

Visitors are responsible for complying with all security instructions, displaying issued identification badges, remaining within authorized areas, protecting company property, and following emergency procedures while on company premises.

Employees serving as hosts are responsible for registering visitors, verifying business necessity, escorting visitors when required, ensuring visitors comply with facility rules, and notifying security when visitors depart.

The Security department is responsible for verifying visitor identity, issuing visitor credentials, maintaining visitor logs, monitoring facility access, responding to security concerns, and enforcing physical access controls.

Facility Management is responsible for maintaining visitor reception areas, physical security infrastructure, surveillance systems, and access control equipment supporting visitor management operations.

## Exceptions

Emergency responders, law enforcement agencies, government regulators, and other authorized officials performing official duties may receive expedited access when permitted by applicable law or organizational emergency procedures. Such access should be documented whenever operationally feasible.

Large corporate events, recruitment drives, training sessions, and public conferences may follow separate visitor registration procedures approved by Security management while maintaining appropriate access controls.

## Compliance

Visitor registration records, access logs, surveillance footage, and badge issuance records may be retained in accordance with the organization's Data Retention Policy and applicable legal requirements. Security personnel may periodically audit visitor records to verify compliance with physical security procedures.

Employees who bypass visitor registration procedures, permit unauthorized access, misuse access badges, fail to escort visitors when required, or otherwise violate this policy may be subject to disciplinary action. Visitors who fail to comply with security instructions may be denied entry, escorted from company premises, or prohibited from future visits.

This policy will be reviewed periodically to ensure continued alignment with physical security standards, regulatory requirements, business operations, and organizational risk management practices.
