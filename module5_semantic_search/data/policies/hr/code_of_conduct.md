# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

The purpose of this Code of Conduct is to define the standards of professional behavior expected from all employees while representing the organization. The policy promotes integrity, accountability, mutual respect, ethical decision-making, and compliance with applicable laws and organizational values. Every employee contributes to maintaining a safe, inclusive, and productive workplace, and adherence to this policy helps preserve the organization's reputation with customers, partners, regulators, and the public.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, officers, directors, interns, contractors, consultants, temporary staff, and any individual representing the organization in a professional capacity. The Code of Conduct applies during working hours, business travel, company-sponsored events, virtual meetings, customer interactions, and communications conducted using company-owned systems or official business accounts.

Employees are expected to comply with this policy regardless of work location, including remote, hybrid, and on-site work environments.

## Policy

Employees shall conduct themselves with honesty, professionalism, and respect in every business interaction. Decisions should be based on ethical principles, organizational policies, and applicable legal requirements rather than personal interests or external influence.

Every employee must treat colleagues, customers, suppliers, and business partners fairly and respectfully. Harassment, discrimination, bullying, intimidation, retaliation, or any form of abusive behavior is strictly prohibited regardless of race, gender, age, disability, religion, nationality, or other legally protected characteristics.

Employees are expected to maintain confidentiality when handling business information, customer records, employee data, financial information, strategic plans, intellectual property, and other sensitive organizational assets. Confidential information must not be disclosed to unauthorized individuals during or after employment unless legally required.

Conflicts of interest must be disclosed immediately to management or Human Resources. Employees should avoid situations where personal relationships, financial interests, or outside employment could influence business decisions or create the appearance of bias.

Company property, including laptops, software, communication systems, office equipment, facilities, and financial resources, must be used responsibly for legitimate business purposes. Misuse, theft, unauthorized access, or intentional damage to company assets may result in disciplinary action.

Employees must communicate professionally through email, messaging platforms, video conferences, social media, and other business communication channels. Offensive language, inappropriate content, or behavior that could negatively affect the organization's reputation is not acceptable.

Gifts, hospitality, or business entertainment should not influence business decisions. Employees must decline gifts or benefits that could reasonably be interpreted as an attempt to gain improper business advantage. Any permissible gifts must comply with company reporting requirements.

Employees are encouraged to report suspected misconduct, unethical behavior, fraud, security violations, policy breaches, or legal non-compliance promptly through approved reporting channels. Reports made in good faith will be treated confidentially to the extent possible.

## Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for understanding this Code of Conduct, completing mandatory compliance training, seeking clarification when uncertain, and demonstrating ethical behavior in daily work activities.

Managers are responsible for leading by example, promoting an ethical workplace culture, addressing concerns promptly, ensuring consistent policy enforcement, and supporting employees who report misconduct in good faith.

Human Resources is responsible for maintaining the Code of Conduct, conducting awareness programs, investigating workplace conduct issues, maintaining confidential records, and recommending corrective actions when policy violations occur.

Senior leadership is responsible for fostering an organizational culture where ethical conduct, transparency, accountability, and compliance are valued across all departments and business operations.

## Exceptions

Limited exceptions may be approved where required by applicable law, contractual obligations, or documented business requirements. Any exception must receive written approval from authorized management and, where appropriate, consultation with Human Resources or the Legal department.

Exceptions shall not permit behavior that compromises employee safety, violates applicable laws, or creates unacceptable ethical or reputational risks for the organization.

## Compliance

Violations of this Code of Conduct will be investigated promptly, fairly, and confidentially. Employees are expected to cooperate fully during investigations by providing accurate information and relevant documentation.

Depending on the nature and severity of the violation, disciplinary measures may include coaching, formal warnings, suspension, reassignment, revocation of system access, termination of employment, legal action, or referral to regulatory authorities where required.

The organization will periodically review this policy to ensure continued alignment with evolving legal requirements, industry standards, organizational values, and best practices in corporate governance. All employees are expected to acknowledge this policy and comply with its provisions throughout their employment.
