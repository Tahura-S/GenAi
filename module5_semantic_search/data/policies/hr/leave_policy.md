# Leave Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this Leave Policy is to provide a consistent framework for managing employee leave while ensuring business continuity and employee well-being. The organization recognizes that planned and unplanned leave is essential for maintaining physical health, mental wellness, family responsibilities, and work-life balance. This policy establishes standardized procedures for requesting, approving, recording, and administering different categories of leave across all departments.

## Scope

This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees unless otherwise specified in an employment agreement or local legal requirement. Temporary staff, interns, contractors, and consultants may be governed by separate contractual terms. Where statutory employment laws provide greater employee benefits than this policy, the applicable legal requirements shall take precedence.

## Policy

Eligible employees receive annual paid leave based on their employment category and length of service. Annual leave is accrued monthly throughout the calendar year and becomes available according to the organization's leave accrual schedule.

Employees are expected to submit planned leave requests through the Human Resources Information System (HRIS) at least seven working days before the intended leave date. Leave requests involving five or more consecutive working days should preferably be submitted at least four weeks in advance to allow adequate workforce planning.

Managers should review leave requests considering operational requirements, project deadlines, staffing availability, and fairness among team members. Approval should not be unreasonably withheld; however, management may propose alternative dates when business continuity could be significantly affected.

Employees should avoid scheduling extended leave during critical business periods, financial year-end activities, major product launches, customer implementation periods, or other designated operational events unless prior approval has been obtained.

Sick leave is intended solely for personal illness, injury, medical treatment, or recovery. Employees must notify their manager as soon as reasonably possible, preferably before the start of their scheduled workday. A medical certificate may be required when sick leave extends beyond three consecutive working days or when repeated short-term absences indicate a pattern requiring further review.

Emergency leave may be granted for unforeseen family emergencies, bereavement, or other exceptional personal circumstances. Employees should notify their reporting manager immediately and provide supporting documentation whenever reasonably possible.

Public holidays recognized by the organization are not deducted from annual leave balances. If a public holiday occurs during an approved vacation period, that day will not count against the employee's annual leave entitlement.

Unused annual leave may be carried forward into the following calendar year, subject to organizational limits. Any leave exceeding the permitted carry-forward balance may expire unless an approved business exception prevented the employee from utilizing their entitlement.

Employees are encouraged to utilize annual leave regularly rather than accumulating excessive balances. Managers share responsibility for ensuring employees take sufficient time away from work to support employee wellness and reduce operational risks associated with employee burnout.

## Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for submitting accurate leave requests, providing appropriate notice whenever possible, maintaining communication during emergency absences, and ensuring work responsibilities are appropriately transitioned before extended leave begins.

Managers are responsible for evaluating leave requests fairly and consistently, balancing employee well-being with departmental operational requirements. Managers should maintain adequate staffing levels, coordinate workload redistribution, and ensure leave approvals comply with organizational policies.

The Human Resources department is responsible for maintaining leave records, administering leave balances, supporting policy interpretation, monitoring compliance, and communicating updates whenever organizational requirements or legal obligations change.

Payroll personnel are responsible for ensuring approved paid leave is accurately reflected in payroll processing and employee leave balances.

## Exceptions

Exceptional business circumstances may require postponement of previously requested leave. Such decisions should be communicated promptly, supported by legitimate operational reasons, and approved by senior management.

Employees experiencing long-term medical conditions, workplace injuries, maternity or paternity events, or legally protected leave situations may be eligible for additional leave provisions under applicable legislation or company benefit programs.

Special leave arrangements may also be approved for educational programs, professional certifications, volunteer initiatives sponsored by the organization, or other circumstances deemed beneficial to both the employee and the organization.

## Compliance

Failure to accurately report leave, misuse sick leave, falsify supporting documentation, or remain absent without authorization may result in disciplinary action, including formal warnings or termination of employment depending on the severity of the violation.

Human Resources will periodically review leave utilization patterns, policy compliance, and approval consistency across departments. Policy audits may be conducted to ensure fairness, regulatory compliance, and continuous improvement of leave administration processes.

Employees with questions regarding leave eligibility, documentation requirements, or special circumstances should consult Human Resources before making leave-related decisions.
