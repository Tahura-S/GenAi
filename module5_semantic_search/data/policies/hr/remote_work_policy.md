# Remote Work Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this Remote Work Policy is to establish clear guidelines for employees who perform their duties outside company premises. The organization supports flexible work arrangements where they improve productivity, employee well-being, and business continuity while maintaining operational efficiency, information security, and collaboration. Remote work is considered a workplace flexibility option rather than an employee entitlement and may be approved only when it aligns with business needs.

## Scope

This policy applies to eligible full-time and part-time employees whose job responsibilities can be effectively performed from a remote location. Positions requiring physical interaction with customers, specialized equipment, secure facilities, or on-site operational support may not qualify for remote work. Contractors, consultants, interns, and temporary staff may follow separate contractual arrangements.

Employees working remotely from locations outside their approved work region must obtain prior approval from Human Resources, Information Technology, and their department manager to ensure compliance with legal, tax, security, and operational requirements.

## Policy

Remote work may be approved as a regular hybrid arrangement, a temporary business requirement, or an exceptional accommodation based on organizational needs. Standard hybrid schedules generally allow employees to work remotely up to three days per week unless otherwise approved.

Employees are expected to maintain the same level of professionalism, productivity, responsiveness, and accountability while working remotely as they would in the office. Business objectives, project deadlines, service quality, and performance expectations remain unchanged regardless of work location.

Employees must be available during designated core business hours, typically between 10:00 AM and 4:00 PM local time. Meetings, customer interactions, training sessions, and collaborative activities should be attended unless approved leave has been granted.

Remote employees must work from a safe, quiet, and professional environment that supports confidential conversations and uninterrupted productivity. Public spaces such as cafés, airports, or shared workspaces should only be used when appropriate security measures can be maintained.

Company-issued laptops and approved hardware should be used whenever possible. Personal devices may only be used after receiving authorization from the Information Technology department and meeting all corporate security requirements.

Employees must connect to internal company systems exclusively through approved Virtual Private Network (VPN) services. Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for all remote access to business applications, cloud platforms, email systems, and confidential resources.

Sensitive company information must not be stored permanently on personal devices, external storage media, or unauthorized cloud storage services. Employees are responsible for protecting confidential documents from unauthorized viewing by family members, visitors, or members of the public.

Managers may require employees to attend in-person meetings, customer engagements, team workshops, performance reviews, training programs, or emergency business activities regardless of their regular remote work schedule. Employees are expected to comply with reasonable attendance requests.

## Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for maintaining reliable internet connectivity, ensuring their workspace supports productive work, protecting company equipment, following cybersecurity requirements, and reporting technical issues promptly.

Managers are responsible for evaluating remote work eligibility, monitoring employee performance based on measurable outcomes, maintaining effective team communication, scheduling regular check-ins, and ensuring remote employees remain integrated into departmental activities.

The Information Technology department is responsible for providing secure remote access, maintaining VPN infrastructure, deploying security updates, monitoring endpoint compliance, and assisting employees with approved technical issues.

Human Resources is responsible for maintaining remote work agreements, communicating policy updates, supporting managers with policy interpretation, and ensuring remote work practices remain fair and consistent across departments.

## Exceptions

Temporary exceptions may be granted during public emergencies, natural disasters, transportation disruptions, medical accommodations, or business continuity events. Such exceptions require approval from department leadership and may be reviewed periodically.

Employees experiencing prolonged internet outages, equipment failures, or unsafe working conditions should immediately notify their manager and coordinate alternative work arrangements. Depending on operational needs, employees may be required to report to the office or another approved work location.

Certain highly confidential projects, regulated business activities, or security-sensitive operations may require employees to work exclusively from designated corporate facilities regardless of their standard remote work arrangement.

## Compliance

Failure to comply with remote work requirements, cybersecurity controls, attendance expectations, or confidentiality obligations may result in suspension or revocation of remote work privileges. Serious violations involving unauthorized disclosure of confidential information, misuse of company equipment, or repeated policy breaches may result in disciplinary action, including termination of employment.

The organization reserves the right to review, modify, or discontinue remote work arrangements based on business requirements, employee performance, security considerations, or regulatory obligations. Periodic audits may be conducted to verify compliance with security standards, equipment usage, and operational effectiveness.
